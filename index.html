Seth Gossage

I'm currently a CIERA postdoctoral fellow at the Center for Interdisciplinary Exploration and Research in Astrophysics (CIERA at Northwestern University). My research interests are broadly in stellar evolution, utilizing tools like MESA to model stars and stellar populations to better understand their underlying physics and relation to the Universe. I'm especially interested in topics and phenomena related to stellar rotation, magnetism, and convection.

I'm currently collaborating with members of Prof. Vicky Kalogera's research group, as a developer of the population synthesis code POSYDON. I received my PhD in Astronomy and Astrophysics from Harvard in 2021 and graduated from San Francisco State University in 2015 with a B.S. in Physics (with a concentration in Astronomy).
